What we can do?

Cycling

Cycling is an environmentally friendly mode of transportation that can help to prevent air pollution. When people choose to cycle instead of drive, they reduce the number of cars on the road, which means fewer emissions from cars. Cars emit pollutants such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter, which can contribute to poor air quality. Cycling is a zero-emission form of transportation, meaning that it does not produce any emissions that contribute to air pollution. In addition, cycling is a form of physical activity that promotes cardiovascular health and reduces the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, stroke, and diabetes. By choosing to cycle instead of drive, individuals can help to reduce their carbon footprint and improve air quality in their communities.In addition to helping prevent air pollution, cycling has numerous other benefits for physical and mental health. Cycling is a low-impact form of exercise that is easy on the joints and can be enjoyed by people of all ages and fitness levels. It is a great way to improve cardiovascular health, strengthen muscles, and improve balance and coordination. Regular cycling can also help to reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, stroke, and diabetes. Cycling can also have mental health benefits, as it can help to reduce stress, anxiety, and depression.

Planting Trees

Trees are able to clean the air and absorb harmful airborne particles and gaseous pollutants. Toxins such as nitrogen oxides, ammonia and sulphur dioxide through their leaves, bark and roots. This improves the air quality in the microclimate around the trees and contributes to a healthier and cleaner environment overall. Trees also soak up the harmful carbon dioxide in our atmosphere. In one year, an acre of mature trees absorbs the same volume of carbon monoxide as produced from a 26,000 mile car journey! Aside from cleaning the air for us, trees supply us with fresh oxygen to breathe.Woodlands have proven to be an invaluable asset when it comes to improving air quality and reducing harmful pollutant levels in the atmosphere. This is why we should try our best to ensure that our most valuable woodlands, like the rainforests, aren't being lost to deforestation. Many areas of rainforest are being sold off to the highest bidder, their beautiful trees (and the important habitat they create for wildlife) being destroyed to make way for areas to graze cattle (reared for beef) or grow unsustainable palm (to create palm oil). When going about your daily life, try to support brands who choose not to work in association with deforestation. Instead, look for foods that use sustainably sourced palm oil and use the Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil (RSPO) logo.

Proper disposal of waste

Proper disposal of waste can help reduce air pollution in several ways. One of the major ways that proper waste disposal helps reduce air pollution is by reducing the emissions from waste incineration. When waste is burned in incinerators, it releases harmful gases and particulate matter into the air, such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, sulfur dioxide, and particulate matter. These pollutants can have serious health effects on people living near the incinerators and contribute to the overall air pollution in the surrounding areas.Another way proper waste disposal can reduce air pollution is by reducing the amount of methane gas released into the atmosphere. Landfills are a major source of methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change. When organic waste, such as food scraps and yard waste, decomposes in a landfill, it releases methane into the atmosphere. Proper waste disposal methods, such as composting and recycling, can help divert organic waste from landfills and reduce the amount of methane released.

Using Eco Friendly Products

Using eco-friendly products can help to reduce air pollution in several ways. One of the most significant ways is by reducing the emissions during manufacturing. Eco-friendly products are typically made from sustainable and renewable materials that require less energy and resources to produce. This can help to reduce the emissions of greenhouse gases and other pollutants during the manufacturing process. In contrast, the production of conventional products often involves the use of fossil fuels and other non-renewable resources, leading to higher emissions of greenhouse gases and other pollutants.Finally, using eco-friendly products can help to reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills. Conventional products are often made with non-biodegradable materials that take a long time to break down in landfills, contributing to air pollution through the release of methane gas. In contrast, many eco-friendly products are made with biodegradable materials that break down more quickly in landfills, reducing the amount of methane gas released into the atmosphere.
